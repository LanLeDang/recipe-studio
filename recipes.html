Loaded Potato Skins

Ingredients

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 4 slices cooked bacon, chopped
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onions
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Bake potatoes for about 45–50 minutes until tender.
  3. Cut potatoes in half and scoop out most of the flesh.
  4. Brush skins with olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  5. Bake skins for 10 minutes until crispy.
  6. Fill with cheese and bacon, then bake again until cheese melts.
  7. Top with sour cream and green onions before serving.

Crab Rangoons

Ingredients

  • 15 square wonton wrappers
  • 1 cup (8 oz) cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup (4 oz) imitation crab, shredded and chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 green onion, sliced
  • Neutral oil, for frying
  • Sweet chili sauce, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix together the cream cheese, imitation crab, garlic powder, soy sauce, and green onion. Taste and adjust if needed.
  2. Wet the edges of a wonton wrapper with water. Place a small spoonful of filling in the center, then fold all four corners together and pinch to seal. Repeat with the remaining wrappers, keeping both the wrappers and folded rangoons covered with a damp towel so they do not dry out.
  3. Heat neutral oil in a pot or fryer to 350°F. Fry the crab rangoons in batches for about 3 to 4 minutes, or until golden and crispy. Do not overcrowd the pot.
  4. Remove the rangoons and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  5. Serve hot with sweet chili sauce.

Animal Style Smashburger Tacos

Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup mayonnaise
  • 3 tablespoons ketchup
  • 2 tablespoons sweet pickle relish
  • 2 tablespoons minced red onion
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onions
  • 1/2 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 8 small flour tortillas
  • 3/4 pound 80/20 ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon yellow mustard
  • Kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 8 slices American cheese
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • Sliced dill pickles
  • 1 small Campari tomato, thinly sliced
  • 1 romaine heart, finely shredded

Instructions

  1. Make the spread by whisking together the mayonnaise, ketchup, pickle relish, red onion, white vinegar, and sugar in a small bowl. Cover and refrigerate until ready to use.
  2. Press the ground beef thinly onto each tortilla, spreading it all the way to the edges. Spread a little yellow mustard on top of the beef, then season with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat a large cast-iron skillet over high heat. Place the tortillas meat-side down into the hot pan and firmly press so the beef cooks flat against the skillet.
  4. Cook for about 1 minute, then scrape and flip the tortilla briefly to warm the other side. Remove and immediately place a slice of American cheese on top. Repeat with the remaining tacos.
  5. Add the diced onion to the skillet and cook in the rendered beef fat until soft and lightly browned, about 8 minutes.
  6. Assemble each taco with a spoonful of spread, grilled onions, pickles, tomato, and shredded lettuce. Serve hot.

Korean Fried Chicken

Ingredients

  • Boneless chicken thighs
  • Cornstarch
  • Neutral oil, for frying
  • Dry sherry, white wine, or rice vinegar
  • Soy sauce
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Onion powder
  • Black Pepper
  • Garlic powder
  • Curry powder
  • Baking powder
  • Cold water

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix the marinade ingredients together, then add the chicken and toss well to coat. Let it marinate briefly, or longer in the fridge for more flavor.
  2. In another bowl, whisk together the batter ingredients until thick but still pourable.
  3. Add the marinated chicken to the batter and coat each piece evenly.
  4. Heat oil in a deep skillet, wok, or heavy pot to about 350°F.
  5. Carefully lower the chicken into the hot oil in small batches and fry until golden brown and crisp, turning as needed. The Kitchn snippet indicates about 8 minutes for pieces in hot oil; the similar full recipe source gives longer times depending on cut size.
  6. Transfer the fried chicken to a wire rack or paper towel-lined tray to drain excess oil.
  7. Serve hot.

Chocolate-Covered Strawberry Yogurt Clusters

Ingredients

  • 1 cup strawberries, diced
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips
  • 2 teaspoons coconut oil

Instructions

  1. Line a large baking sheet or tray with parchment paper.
  2. Add the diced strawberries to a large bowl. Mix in the Greek yogurt and vanilla extract until the strawberries are well coated.
  3. Scoop the mixture onto the lined baking sheet, using about 2 tablespoons for each cluster. Leave space between each one. You should get about 8 clusters.
  4. Place the tray in the freezer for 30 to 45 minutes, until the clusters are firm.
  5. Near the end of the freezing time, add the chocolate chips and coconut oil to a small bowl. Microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring between each, until smooth.
  6. Remove the clusters from the freezer. Using a spoon, quickly dip or coat each cluster in the melted chocolate, then place it back on the baking sheet.
  7. Let the clusters sit for a few minutes until the chocolate hardens, then serve.

Che Thai

Ingredients

  • 1 can water chestnuts, cut into small cubes
  • 2–3 drops red food coloring
  • 2 cups tapioca starch
  • 2 1/2 cups half-and-half
  • 1/2 to 3/4 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can lychee or longan
  • 1 can rambutan
  • 1 can coconut jelly
  • 1 can aiyu jelly, cut into cubes
  • 1 can toddy palm seed and jackfruit

Instructions

  1. Place the cubed water chestnuts in a bowl with the red food coloring and toss until evenly coated. Let them sit for 5 to 10 minutes.
  2. Add the tapioca starch to a large bowl, then toss the colored water chestnuts in the starch until fully coated. Shake off the excess starch.
  3. Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the coated chestnuts and boil until they float, about 1 to 2 minutes. Transfer them to an ice bath and set aside.
  4. In a large bowl or pitcher, whisk together the half-and-half and sweetened condensed milk until smooth. Chill until ready to use.
  5. Drain all the canned fruits and jellies. Cut any larger pieces, like the aiyu jelly or jackfruit, into smaller pieces if needed.
  6. In serving bowls or cups, add the red rubies and your choice of fruits and jellies. Pour the sweet milk mixture over the top.
  7. Add crushed ice if desired, then serve immediately or chill for 1 to 2 hours before serving.
